EEG Markers of Consciousness in Unresponsive Patients

This paper explores the diagnostic and prognostic value of EEG markers in assessing consciousness in unresponsive patients, emphasizing the significance of etiology-specific analysis and revealing the varied impact of EEG features across different patient groups.

Term Definition
EEG Electroencephalogram; a test used to evaluate the electrical activity in the brain.
Anoxic Relating to or marked by a lack of oxygen.
Etiology The cause or origin of a disease or condition.
Spectral Power The square of the amplitude of the EEG signal for specific frequency bands.
Spectral Exponent A parameter describing the decay of power spectral density with frequency.
PAR (Posterior-Anterior Ratio) Compares power of EEG signals from the back to the front of the brain.
Lempel-Ziv Complexity A measure of the complexity or randomness of a signal.

Phase-normalized Normalization technique based on randomizing the phase of a signal while preserving its amplitude spectrum.
